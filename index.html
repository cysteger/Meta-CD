Meta-CD: Metagenomic Sequencing Coverage and Depth Calculator

This tool is designed for species‑specific metagenomic evaluation. Allowing researchers to estimate the sequencing coverage, required depth, minimum detectable abundance, and MAG recovery potential for an individual microbial species; based on its genome size, relative abundance, sequencing depth, and sample DNA quantity.

DNA quantity (ng) Limits effective depth

DNA Quantity Explained

DNA quantity determines the maximum possible unique sequencing depth. Even if you sequence 50 Gb, you cannot exceed the number of unique molecules in the sample. This calculator uses DNA quantity as a hard cap on effective depth.

  • 1 ng DNA ≈ 1 Gbp of unique sequence
  • Effective depth = min(nominal depth, DNA‑limited depth)
  • Low DNA input limits achievable coverage
  • Rare organisms are affected most strongly
  • MAG recovery becomes impossible if DNA is too low

Example: 2 ng DNA + 20 Gb sequencing → effective depth = 2 Gb.

Coverage Thresholds Explained

Target coverage for minimum RA is the minimum sequencing coverage required for an organism to be considered detectable. This value determines the coverage threshold used when calculating the minimum relative abundance needed for detection. Higher thresholds provide more confident detection but require higher abundance or deeper sequencing.

  • 1×-5x — Minimal coverage required; permissive and shallow coverage.
  • 10×-30x — Strongly recommended; higher confidence and deeper coverage.

Pre‑Sequencing Estimation

These outputs describe what sequencing depth is required to reach a target coverage or what minimum abundance is detectable at a given depth.

Required Sequencing Depth

Uses target coverage, relative abundance, genome size, and DNA quantity and calculates how much sequencing is needed to reach a desired coverage level for a given organism. Lower abundance organisms require disproportionately more sequencing to reach the same coverage. DNA quantity limits the maximum achievable depth, so high target coverage may not be possible.

Minimum Relative Abundance Required

Determines the minimum abundance at which an organism can be detected or assembled at a desired coverage. Uses target coverage, genome size, sequencing depth, and DNA quantity. Lower sequencing depth or larger genome size increases the required abundance. DNA quantity limits the effective depth, raising the minimum abundance needed for detection.

Footnote — Interpreting MAG Recovery Requirements

To successfully recover high‑quality Metagenome‑Assembled Genomes (MAGs), the amount of sequencing required depends on sample complexity and the abundance of target organisms. Typical recommended sequencing depths per sample are:

Suggested Data Outputs
• Low complexity (e.g., simplified mock communities): 5–10 Gb (~10–15M reads)
• Moderate complexity (e.g., wastewater): 10–20 Gb (~20–30M reads)
• High complexity (e.g., soil): 30–50+ Gb (~60M+ reads)

Key Factors for MAG Recovery
• De novo assembly requires deep sequencing — far more than taxonomic profiling.
• Per‑genome coverage of ~10×–30× is typically needed for reliable MAG assembly.
• Abundance matters — dominant organisms assemble at lower depths; rare taxa require much more data.
• Long‑read platforms (PacBio HiFi, ONT) often achieve high‑quality MAGs with ~10–15 Gb due to longer contigs.

How These Calculations Work

1. Bases sequenced for the species
Total sequencing depth is measured in gigabases (Gb), where 1 Gb = 109 bases. If a species has relative abundance A (e.g., 1% = 0.01), then the number of bases sequenced for that species is: 

Species bases = (D × 10⁹) × A

Where; D = Total sequencing depth (in gigabases, Gb).

2. Genome size conversion
Genome size G is entered in megabases (Mb), where 1 Mb = 106 bases. 

Genome bases = G × 10⁶

3. Coverage formula
Coverage is defined as the number of sequenced bases divided by the genome size: 

Coverage = (D × 10⁹ × A) / (G × 10⁶)
         = (D × 1000 × A) / G

In this tool, D is replaced by the DNA‑quantity–adjusted effective depth when DNA is limiting. This is the standard definition of sequencing coverage used in genomics and metagenomics.

4. Required sequencing depth for a target coverage
Rearranging the coverage formula gives: 

Required depth (Gb) = (Coverage × G) / (1000 × A)

This is commonly used in metagenomic sampling‑depth calculations for MAG recovery.
