How These Calculations Work

This page provides the mathematical framework used by Meta‑CD to compute WGS coverage, required sequencing depth, minimum detectable abundance, and MAG recovery potential. All calculations follow standard metagenomic coverage theory with explicit incorporation of DNA‑input constraints.

Core Coverage Model

Estimating the sequencing depth required to achieve a target coverage for a specific microbial genome follows directly from the standard definition of coverage in metagenomics. Coverage (C) is defined as the ratio of the total number of bases sequenced from a species to the size of its genome. When the genome size, relative abundance, and desired coverage are known, the required community sequencing depth can be solved algebraically.

C = Dspecies / G

Where G is the genome size (Mb) and Dspecies is the total number of bases sequenced from that organism.

DNA‑Limited Effective Depth

Meta‑CD incorporates DNA‑input constraints using:

Deffective = min(Dcommunity, Dmax)

Where:

Dmax = DNAng

This reflects the empirical approximation that 1 ng DNA ≈ 1 Gbp of unique sequence. If sequencing depth exceeds this limit, coverage becomes DNA‑limited.

Where Dcommunity Comes From

In metagenomics, sequencing effort is typically reported as total community sequencing depth, measured in gigabases (Gb). This value represents the total number of bases generated across the entire microbial community:

Dcommunity (bases) = Dcommunity (Gb) × 109

Because shotgun sequencing samples DNA fragments approximately in proportion to their abundance, the number of bases originating from a species is:

Dspecies = Deffective × 1000 × A

The factor of 1000 converts gigabases to megabases, and A is relative abundance (fraction).

Required Sequencing Depth

To determine how much sequencing is needed to reach a target coverage, the coverage equation is solved for community depth:

Drequired = (Ctarget × G) / (1000 × A)

This value increases linearly with genome size and desired coverage, and inversely with abundance. If the required depth exceeds the DNA‑limited maximum, the target coverage is not achievable with the available DNA.

Minimum Relative Abundance Required

The minimum abundance needed to reach a target coverage is obtained by solving the coverage equation for A:

Amin = (Ctarget × G) / (1000 × Deffective)

Lower sequencing depth or larger genomes increase the required abundance. DNA‑limited samples have higher Amin because Deffective is capped.

Bases Sequenced for This Species

The expected number of bases originating from a species is:

Bspecies = Dcommunity × 109 × A

This determines how deeply a species is represented in the dataset and forms the basis for all coverage‑based analyses.

Coverage From Sequencing Depth

Substituting abundance‑scaled depth into the coverage equation yields:

C = (Deffective × 1000 × A) / G

Coverage increases with sequencing depth and abundance, and decreases with genome size. DNA‑limited samples exhibit proportionally reduced coverage.

MAG Recovery Potential

Metagenome-assembled genome (MAG) recovery depends primarily on the sequencing coverage achieved for each organism in the community. Coverage determines the extent to which genomic regions are represented in the sequencing data, influencing both the contiguity and completeness of assembled genomes. Because shotgun metagenomic sequencing distributes reads approximately in proportion to the underlying taxonomic composition, MAG recovery potential can be estimated directly from the expected coverage of each species.

Coverage (C) for a species is computed as:

C = (Deffective × 1000 × A) / G

where Deffective is the sequencing depth after applying any DNA quantity constraints, A is the relative abundance of the organism (fraction), G is the genome size in megabases (Mb), and the factor of 1000 converts gigabases (Gb) to megabases (Mb). This formulation is consistent with standard definitions of metagenomic coverage and provides a quantitative basis for evaluating the feasibility of genome reconstruction.

MAG recovery likelihood is interpreted using coverage thresholds:

  • >20× — high likelihood
  • 10–20× — moderate
  • 1–5× — low
  • <1× — very unlikely

These thresholds reflect typical assembly performance across metagenomic datasets and provide a practical guide for evaluating genome reconstruction feasibility.

Coverage Estimation Table

The coverage estimation table applies the same coverage formula across a grid of sequencing depths and abundances, incorporating DNA‑limited depth where applicable. It provides a rapid reference for evaluating feasibility of detection, profiling, and assembly.

Table Summary of Formulas

Mathematical calculations used in Meta‑CD to compute species‑specific sequencing bases, coverage, detection limits, and required sequencing depth. Variables are defined as follows:

Output Formula / Calculation Explanation Ref
Dcommunity (Base conversion) Dcommunity (bases) = Dcommunity (Gb) × 109 Total sequencing depth converted from gigabases to bases. (5)
Maximum Unique Depth (DNA-limited) Dmax = DNAng Approximation that 1 ng DNA yields ~1 Gbp of unique sequence. (7,10)
Species‑Specific Sequencing Bases Bspecies = Dcommunity × 109 × A Total number of DNA bases sequenced from target species. Sequencing depth (in Gb) is converted to bases and multiplied by relative abundance of target species. (3,5)
Coverage From Sequencing Depth C = (Deffective × 1000 × A) / G Expected average coverage for a genome of size G at abundance A, using effective depth adjusted for DNA-input constraint. (5,11)
Effective Sequencing Depth (DNA-Limited) Deffective = min(Dtotal, Dmax) If DNA input constrains library complexity, the effective depth is capped at Dmax (1 ng ≈ 1 Gb DNA base pairs). (7,10)
Required Sequencing Depth Drequired = (Ctarget × G) / (1000 × A) Sequencing depth needed to reach target coverage for a species at a given relative abundance. (3,5,11)
Minimum Detectable Relative Abundance Amin = (Ctarget × G) / (1000 × Deffective) Lowest relative abundance at which the species reaches the target coverage under the available effective depth. (3,5,11)
